The main points of the lecture were
  • Diffusion with reaction in liquids
  • Hole transfer in Sb2S3 solar cells

Goals:

After this class you should be able to:
  • Know what the Hatta number is.
  • Understand how a first order consumption reaction affects both steady-state and transient diffusion problems.
  • Figure out how to extract diffusivities and mass transfer coefficients from transient concentration measurements.

Problem of the Day:

    In this problem we examine the related problem of gas absorption from a sparger: the idea is that you release bubbles of gas with total surface area A into a well-mixed volume V. The gas dissolves into the liquid and reacts away, with the mass transfer limitation being approximated by diffusion through a thin film at the surface of the bubble.
